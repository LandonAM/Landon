  • Horses are terrifying

    A druid from a remote, cold land has joined our party in Curse of Strahd. We’re traveling on a creepy road when the sound of horse hooves comes from behind us.

    Druid: What is that sound?

    Monk (Me): You’ve never heard a horse before??

    Druid: What’s a horse??

    We run off the road to avoid the horseman, who turns out to be a skeleton rider on a rotting skeleton horse. He stops to look at us, then rides off into the mist. We wait a bit then get back on the road.

    Monk (Me): (to the druid) So you've never seen a horse before?

    Druid: I’ve heard of them, but no. Where I come from we have bears. And wolves.

    Monk (Me): So that was the first horse you’ve ever seen?

    Druid: Yes.

    Monk (Me): That’s crazy! Everyone rides horses.

    Druid: *thinking of the rotting skeleton horse he just saw* Horses are terrifying.

    We tried to explain that not all horses are like that, but as of now he’s convinced that everyone rides nightmarish undead creatures in this land.

  • Papers please

    Background: our party is in a group of islands that are separate from the main continents and is its own government set up with each island acting like a separate state or kingdom. My character (the half-Orc bard) went to a local lord we befriend to ask about this magic purest cult called the order in the world.

    Me: So what is up with this rule that certain people should use magic and that certain spells shouldn’t be used?

    Lord: Yes, it because of the order. Didn’t you learn about it growing up?

    Me: No, they never really came to my tribe. I’m guessing it’s because we didn’t have magic users and it would have been too much effort to make it through the snows. I mean there were those weird guys who would always take the elder off to talk in private when we made trading runs but I never had to interact with them.

    Lord: Wait, where are you from?

    Me: Oh, the southern continent! My tribe was at the top of one of the snowy mountains, just beyond the tree line! I forgot what you called it in common but we called it “Ice home” in orc.

    Lord: Oh! I’m surprised they didn’t talk to you about it when you got to the islands and got your papers. You would have come into port in one of their most controlled cities.

    Me: … papers?

    Lord: Yes, you know, the travel papers that show to them you’re allowed on the island? You know, the ones they make everyone that comes to the island fill out at the port? You did come by boat correct?

    Me: … Yes.

    Lord: Then how did you not know about the papers?

    Me: … because I never got any?

    Lord: … W-what?!? But how did you get past them?

    Me: … they didn’t know I was there? I didn’t have money and I didn’t think the islands were real till I got to town and people were talking about it so I sort of stowed away. I didn’t know there were papers? No one ever told me. And I thought the islands were just a fairly tail till, well, I was standing on them.

    Lord: … by the gods you guys are lucky that you haven’t had to show them especially with how well known you’ve become on the local islands. I… I’ll take care of this, I can get the papers fast forwarded, I’ll push them through, need to pull a few favors but I’m sure with all you guys have done we can do it.


    Mean while the party is all yelling at me that you don’t just admit to this.


    (None of us knew of the papers. They were all born on the inner islands where it’s not a thing really because the ships have to land on other islands first.)
